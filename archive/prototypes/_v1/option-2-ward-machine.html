The Ward Machine

An electoral field organiser is assigned a target ward they've never set foot in. The machine finds the issue that moves voters there — and writes the doorstep script. The most frightening story of the four.

User: party field organiser (party unnamed)
Issue: found by the machine — school street, Castle ward, Leicester
Factory axis: one party × every battleground ward
Framing: electoral
Fear Message: volunteer displacement · issues chosen for electoral utility
Real-world anchor (verified): Castle ward, Leicester — covers Clarendon Park — is Green-held since May 2023 (Kitterick 1,564 · Gregg 1,453 · Sahu 1,419, turnout 30.4%), all seats up again May 2027, in Leicester South (MP: Shockat Adam, Ind). A genuinely contested ward with a genuinely live hyperlocal issue. Better still (verified): the scheme straddles the Castle/Knighton boundary — Green-held Castle on one side, Labour-held Knighton on the other, and the councillor fronting the scheme (Geoff Whittle, Assistant City Mayor) sits for Knighton. A real two-party fault line runs down the middle of the school street. The storyboard uses this real terrain with synthetic campaign numbers.

Honest assessment

Strengths: exactly matches the audience (political campaigners) and Ed's original notes (“650 CLPs… strategy centrally, agents locally… maybe you'll need less volunteers”). The only option where the fear is native to the story rather than bolted on. The review-flag moment (machine invents social proof, human catches it) is the strongest single approval-gate beat of any option. Feeds every panel theme: centralisation, displacement, synthetic campaigning, surveillance of public records.
Weaknesses / risks: ethically hottest — instrumentalises a real school's road-safety scheme for electoral gain, with a named real ward and real incumbents on screen; must be staged as “look straight at what's coming”, never as a product pitch. Party must stay unnamed. “Voter resonance” scoring is methodologically fake and must be labelled as such on stage or it becomes the thing we're warning about. Needs Ed and Hannah's explicit sign-off on political heat.
