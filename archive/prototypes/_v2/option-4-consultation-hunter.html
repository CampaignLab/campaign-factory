Presenter

About this concept

The Consultation Hunter — tracks every open UK consultation and drafts responses before deadlines.

User & problem

Any small advocacy org. Statutory decision windows open and close without the people affected ever hearing — verified: no aggregator of UK local-authority consultations exists. This machine is the thing that's missing, which is both the pitch and the warning.

Central demonstration idea

Timing, not content. The emotional engine is a true story: a school-street consultation in Brent closed four days before this research ran, unannounced. Then the machine catches Leicester's live objection window with 112 days to spare.

Main flow

Model guesses (wrong) → live feeds polled (3 keyless APIs) → deadline triage: the miss, then the catch → drafted response with a disclosed source discrepancy → human approval → 1,400-consultation national view.

What's real

LiveGOV.UK, Citizen Space and Go Vocal feed pulls; ranking; drafting — the most defensible live layer of the four options
CachedBrent record, TfL Barnes lifecycle, Leicester TRO documents
Deterministicplatform adapters, deadline arithmetic, citation binding, submission blocking
Agenticrelevance ranking against org goals; drafting; discrepancy handling
Simulatedthe 1,400 national board (per-platform feeds real; the 317-council crawl is not built — labelled on screen)

Benefits

Strongest verified data spine; genuinely useful after the conference; the sharpest closing question about participation.

Risks & tensions

Least agentic — the power is plumbing, and the demo says so honestly. “Consultation” needs its first 90 seconds to make the audience feel stakes; the Brent miss does that job.

Build complexity

Medium-low: three adapters + triage + drafting. Kit mirrors Base UI; production swaps to @base-ui-components/react.

In a 10-minute demo

Very reliable, quietly devastating. If rehearsal audiences don't react to the Brent miss, the standalone story fails — fold the radar into the winning option as one panel.
