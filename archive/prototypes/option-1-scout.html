Prototype 1 · Issue advocacy · Leicester LE2

A research department for one campaigner

What it is: enter a postcode; get the local issue, cited evidence, and a draft campaign. How it uses an agent factory: the question fans out to specialist read-only agents — scout, verifier, context, drafter, reviewer — coordinated by an orchestrator that runs whole batches in the cloud and returns finished packs to a human inbox. This page walks the six rungs from a chat answer to a 412-run factory.

4

Specialist agents

Roles split: each agent has its own prompt, tools, and context window. The reviewer doesn't share the drafter's context — that separation is what catches the lie.

5 specialist agents

Specialists can lose shared context; the pipeline passes structured evidence, not vibes, to compensate.

A lead agent decomposes the job, runs specialists in parallel, and synthesises the pack. This is where “an agent” becomes “a system”.

The factory

Add a queue, cloud workers, and an inbox — and it runs without anyone typing. Input arrives (a seed list of 412 schools), packs return, humans approve or reject, and rejections re-enter the queue with notes.

About this concept

User & problem

Sam, the sole campaigner at a two-person road-safety charity. Finding a genuine local issue takes a week of council-website archaeology; the factory compresses it to minutes, with citations, at national scale.

Where the agents multiply — and why it isn't theatre

Rungs 1–3 are one model with better plumbing. Agents genuinely multiply at rung 4 for three defensible reasons: parallelism (read-heavy research over separable sources), context isolation (each specialist burns its own window on its own documents), and reviewer/generator separation (the checker must not share the writer's context). Rung 6 adds the factory properties: queue, triggers, cloud sandboxes, evaluator, human-in-the-loop gate with approve/edit/reject/respond, and a trace per run.

Risks & tensions

Gentlest reveal of the four prototypes — capacity, not menace; the panel supplies the unease. Approval-at-scale is the honest worry: the gate is real, but attention doesn't scale like compute.

Build complexity & 10-minute fit

Medium: full pipeline, one location, all critical sources keyless. Clearest teaching version of the ladder; weakest closing note — consider the hybrid ending. UI mirrors shadcn (Awake template tokens); production would use shadcn/ui React components directly.
