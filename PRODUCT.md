# Product

## Register

product

## Users

UK local and public-policy campaigners, funders, and campaign technologists. The primary user is a campaigner reviewing a hyperlocal campaign, its evidence, strategy, and practical resources; the conference audience must also understand the capability quickly during a live demonstration.

## Product Purpose

Campaign Factory turns a local campaign problem into a connected, evidence-aware campaign journey and practical resources. Mission Bay extends that campaign by making coordinated agent missions visible, reviewable, and bounded by human political judgement. Success means campaigners understand both the immediate utility and the unsettling institutional capacity of an agent factory without mistaking simulation, inference, or automation for verified political reality.

## Brand Personality

Direct, intelligent, provocative. The product should feel rigorous enough to trust, ambitious enough to provoke discussion, and plain-spoken enough for non-technical campaigners.

## Anti-references

- A generic AI chatbot or prompt library.
- A permanent operations console or monitoring dashboard.
- An agent marketplace filled with interchangeable personas.
- A literal digital-workforce fantasy that anthropomorphises model calls.
- A dense architecture diagram with the campaign hidden underneath it.
- A theatrical activity feed that implies work which did not occur.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the campaign outcome; reveal orchestration as supporting evidence.
2. Make provenance, uncertainty, inference, simulation, and approval states unmistakable.
3. Show coordination through real dependencies, disagreement, and synthesis rather than inflated agent counts.
4. Preserve hyperlocal specificity; factory scale comes from disciplined machinery, not copied local variants.
5. Keep meaningful political decisions and external actions under human control.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All status and provenance distinctions must work without colour alone; controls require visible keyboard focus; animated orchestration must respect reduced-motion preferences; live mission events require text equivalents and accessible announcements; language must remain understandable to campaigners without technical agent-system knowledge.
